TornadoCash Overview: zk Design, Mixer Flows & TornadoCash Nova

TornadoCash is a non-custodial privacy protocol using zero-knowledge proofs to unlink deposit and withdrawal addresses. The TornadoCash Mixer relies on fixed denominations and Merkle-tree membership proofs to preserve a uniform anonymity set. TornadoCash Nova historically explored improved UX/throughput; the core principles remain: proofs, nullifiers, and careful opsec.

Core Components in TornadoCash Mixer

  • Commitments: a hash of a secret + nullifier stored in a Merkle tree during deposit.
  • Nullifiers: revealed at withdrawal to prevent double spending while keeping deposits unlinkable.
  • Relayers: optional submitters paying gas so your withdrawal address is not linked to the funding source.

Best Execution of Privacy — TornadoCash

  • Anonymity first: wait for a healthy set; avoid unique timing/amount patterns.
  • Effective privacy: optimize for net unlinkability, not speed; consider delayed withdrawal and relayers.
  • Reliability: track notes securely; test with a small denomination before moving size.

Security & Operational Safety with TornadoCash

TornadoCash improves on-chain privacy but cannot guarantee absolute anonymity. Deanonymization via timing analysis, dusting, address reuse, or phishing remains possible. Treat keys and notes as production secrets.

Wallet, Notes & Allowance Hygiene

  • Use a hardware wallet for deposits/withdrawals; verify spender and exact amounts on-device.
  • Store notes/secrets offline; without the note, you cannot withdraw.
  • Avoid reusing funding or destination identities; separate personas and RPC endpoints if possible.

Threat Model & Mitigations (TornadoCash)

  • Timing correlation: stagger deposit/withdraw windows; let the set grow first.
  • Amount matching: fixed denomination helps; avoid rare denominations when possible.
  • Gas linkability: consider relayers; do not fund the withdrawal address from the deposit wallet.

Key Functions: TornadoCash, TornadoCash Mixer & TornadoCash Nova

Canonical Flow in TornadoCash Mixer

Generate secret/nullifier → deposit a fixed denomination → wait for more deposits → withdraw with a zk proof (revealing only a nullifier hash). Optionally route via a relayer to hide gas funding.

Relayers & OpSec on TornadoCash

  • Choose reputable relayers; verify addresses and fees; avoid phishing/imitations.
  • Consider delayed withdrawal or multi-note strategies to diversify timing.
  • Monitor pool activity to estimate anonymity-set health before withdrawing.

Runbook: Reliable, Responsible Use of TornadoCash

Before You Start (TornadoCash)

  • Back up secrets/notes offline (paper/air-gapped device).
  • Verify pool/contract addresses; bookmark official links.
  • Test with a small denomination first; confirm withdraw works end-to-end.

Denominations, Timing & Nova

  • Prefer common denominations to maximize the anonymity set.
  • Stagger withdrawals; avoid unique timings (e.g., immediately after deposit).
  • TornadoCash Nova: focus on UX/throughput concepts; still apply strict opsec and note management.

Troubleshooting — TornadoCash Mixer

  • Proof failed / verification error: regenerate proof with correct Merkle root; ensure note integrity.
  • Stuck tx: increase priority fee or change RPC; check mempool congestion.
  • Relayer unavailable: switch to a vetted alternative, or fund a clean wallet to self-submit (accepting linkability trade-offs).
